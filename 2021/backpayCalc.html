Backpay Calculator for 2021 IT TA

How this calculator works

Creating Periods: The calculator divides your employment timeline into 'periods' based on consistent pay rates. A new period starts with any change in pay rate, such as a raise or a new role.

Example: Starting a job at one pay rate and then receiving a raise at a later date creates two distinct periods.

Calculation Process: It calculates your earnings for each period by considering your yearly rate, any acting or unpaid leave. It then compares your actual earnings to what would have been earned under a new pay agreement.

Assumptions and Limitations: Assumes a full-time, regular schedule. Not suitable for part-time or irregular schedules. It doesn't handle specifics like parental leave or different collective agreements.

  • Overtime: You don't need to put every single overtime instance in. Just a total of hours for each rate (1.5 or 2.0) for each period. Example: if you worked for an extra hour every day for a week where your pay rate didn't change, enter any date within that week, and enter 5 for the number of hours, and 1.5x for the rate.
